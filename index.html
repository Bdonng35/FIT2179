THE UNEVEN MALAYSIA HOUSEHOLD REALITY

A visual story of income, poverty, inequality, expenditure, and basic living conditions across Malaysia.

Malaysia has grown richer over time, but growth has not translated into equal household wellbeing everywhere. Income, inequality, expenditure and access to basic amenities reveal a country where development remains uneven across states.

Section 1: Growth

At the national level, Malaysia's household economy has improved substantially over time. Rising income and a growing number of households suggest broad economic progress, but national averages only tell part of the story.

National Household Income Over Time

Mean and median household income have both risen over time, showing that Malaysia has become wealthier overall.

Growth of Households

The increase in the number of households provides demographic context for growth and shows how the country has expanded alongside rising income.

Section 2: Uneven Prosperity

National growth does not mean every state has benefited equally. Looking across states reveals sharp differences in household income, with some places enjoying much higher prosperity than others.

Income Choropleth Map

Mapping household income shows that prosperity is unevenly distributed across Malaysia, with clear geographic patterns of advantage and disadvantage.

State Income Ranking

Ranking states by household income makes the size of these differences easier to compare directly.

These income gaps matter because lower-income states are often more vulnerable to poverty and weaker living conditions.

Section 3: Poverty

Income differences matter because lower prosperity is often tied to greater vulnerability. Poverty data helps show where economic hardship remains concentrated.

Poverty Rate Map

A poverty map reveals where households remain more exposed to economic hardship despite broader national growth.

Income Versus Poverty

Comparing income with poverty shows how strongly prosperity and deprivation are linked across states.

Section 4: Hidden Inequality

Higher income does not always mean households share prosperity evenly. Inequality measures reveal that some places may appear better off overall while still containing deep internal gaps.

Gini Trends Across States

Tracking Gini coefficients over time shows that inequality has changed differently across states rather than following one national pattern.

Income Versus Gini

Comparing income with inequality highlights a key tension: richer states are not necessarily more equal.

Section 5: Cost of Living

Household wellbeing depends not only on income, but also on what households must spend. In some states, higher incomes may be offset by higher living costs.

Income Versus Expenditure

Comparing income and expenditure reveals where higher earnings may come with greater cost pressures.

Expenditure Ranking

Section 6: Living Conditions

Economic outcomes ultimately shape everyday life. Access to water, electricity, and sanitation provides a more human picture of uneven development.

Amenities Comparison

Comparing access to essential services shows that basic living conditions still vary across Malaysia.

Amenities Map

A geographic view of amenities highlights where households continue to face disadvantages in essential services.

Section 7: Distribution

National averages can hide how income is distributed across households. Looking across percentiles reveals who is benefiting most from growth and who remains left behind.

Percentile Distribution

A custom distribution chart shows the shape of household income across the population rather than relying on summary averages.

Conclusion: Growth Is Not the Same as Wellbeing

Malaysia's household story is not simply one of economic growth. While the country has become richer, prosperity remains uneven across states, inequality persists within them, and access to basic living conditions is not uniform. Economic growth does not guarantee equal wellbeing.
